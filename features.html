The Features of Engagement

User engagement, or consumer engagement, has always been a critical topic in advertising. The primary goal of marketing a brand or product is to bring that product to the customer. However, with the rise of social media, the dynamics have shifted towards a more bottom-up driven market. In this new landscape, if we imagine a blank canvas with users as collaborative artists, the true insights into consumer behavior emerge only after users begin to engage with content.

The Complexity of Engagement

Engagement is far from straightforward. Modern research, such as that by Dolan et al. (2019), highlights how factors like the time of day or the day of the week can significantly impact engagement levels. In marketing, the key to capturing attention is appeal—the ability to create a desire for the product. On social media, this translates to fostering active engagement with a brand or product.

Informative Content vs. High-Arousal Appeals

Informative content, which emphasizes the features and benefits of a product, often leads to passive engagement like likes or shares. However, it does not necessarily drive conversation or deeper interaction. In contrast, high-arousal positive appeals—particularly those that are visually compelling—tend to generate more active engagement. The relevance of the visual content to the consumer plays a crucial role in motivating them to engage actively.

The Role of Social Confirmation

Social confirmation, or the influence of seeing others engage with content, plays a significant role in driving further engagement. Once a piece of content reaches a certain level of appeal, engagement can increase exponentially. However, understanding how to achieve this critical level of social confirmation is a challenge. Much of the literature points to the influencer effect as a key driver of appeal and engagement for products.

The Impact of Remunerative Content

Rietveld et al. (2020) emphasize that content which offers rewards or serves a specific purpose tends to generate more positive engagement. Until users perceive a tangible benefit from engaging, the appeal remains limited. One effective strategy for driving remunerative content is leveraging influencers to discuss the topic. Additionally, targeting distinct groups with tailored ads that resonate with their specific interests can increase passive engagement.

Challenges in Driving Active Engagement

Despite these strategies, driving active engagement on social media remains a challenge. One assumption is that people are more likely to discuss topics with those they trust. Building that trust and relevance within specific communities or groups is essential for converting passive observers into active participants.
