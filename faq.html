Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About PMRI

Find answers to frequently asked questions about the Purdue Military Research Institute program, academic requirements, and military obligations.

What is PMRI?

The Purdue Military Research Institute (PMRI) connects military graduate students with cutting-edge research opportunities across disciplines at Purdue University. It provides a platform for Army officers to pursue advanced degrees while maintaining their military obligations.

What are the academic requirements?

PMRI students must maintain full-time graduate student status and meet all academic requirements of their respective departments. This includes maintaining a minimum GPA and making satisfactory progress toward their degree.

How do I apply for PMRI?

The application process involves coordination between your branch manager, the PMRI program office, and your academic department. Contact the PMRI program office for detailed application instructions.

What are the military obligations while in PMRI?

PMRI students must maintain military standards, participate in required training, and stay within the 100-mile radius of West Lafayette unless on approved leave or pass. Regular check-ins with the PMRI cadre are required.

How do I handle leave and passes?

Leave and passes must be coordinated through your chain of command and approved by the PMRI cadre. All absences must be tracked through IPPS-A and should be planned around academic requirements.

What housing options are available?

PMRI students can choose from on-campus housing, off-campus apartments, or military housing if available. The PMRI program office can provide guidance on housing options and BAH considerations.

How do I find a research advisor?

The PMRI program office can help connect you with faculty members whose research aligns with your interests. You should begin this process early in your application.

What happens after graduation?

Upon graduation, you will be assigned to your next duty station based on the needs of the Army. The PMRI program office will coordinate with HRC for your follow-on assignment.
